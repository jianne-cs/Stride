Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
Change your thoughts, change your battles

CBT helps you identify and change negative thought patterns that feed your monsters. The core idea: your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are all connected. By changing one, you change all.

Core Principles

🔺
Cognitive Triangle
Thoughts → Feelings → Behaviors
⚠️
Cognitive Distortions
Identify thinking traps
🔄
Cognitive Restructuring
Replace negative thoughts

CBT Techniques

📝
Thought Record
Write down automatic negative thoughts and challenge them with evidence. Ask: "What's the proof? What's a balanced perspective?"
🔍
Cognitive Distortions
Identify thinking traps: all-or-nothing, overgeneralization, catastrophizing, mind reading, emotional reasoning
⚖️
Evidence Testing
Treat thoughts as hypotheses, not facts. Look for evidence that supports AND contradicts the thought.
🔄
Reframing
Create balanced, realistic alternative thoughts. "I'm not a failure, I just made a mistake."

Common Cognitive Distortions

All-or-nothing thinking Overgeneralization Mental filter Disqualifying positive Jumping to conclusions Magnification Emotional reasoning Should statements Labeling Personalization

Mindfulness
Be present with your monsters, don't become them

Mindfulness is the practice of paying attention to the present moment without judgment. It helps you observe your thoughts and feelings without getting caught up in them.

Core Principles

🎯
Present Moment
Focus on now, not past or future
👁️
Non-judgment
Observe without labeling good/bad
💨
Acceptance
Let thoughts come and go

Mindfulness Exercises

🌬️

Mindful Breathing

Focus on your breath—notice the sensation of inhaling and exhaling. When your mind wanders, gently bring it back.

⏱️ 5 minutes 🎯 Beginner
🌍

Body Scan

Slowly bring attention to each part of your body, from head to toe, noticing sensations without judgment.

⏱️ 10 minutes 🎯 Intermediate
👂

Mindful Listening

Pick a sound and listen to it fully—music, nature sounds, or even silence. Notice the layers and textures.

⏱️ 3 minutes 🎯 Beginner
🚶

Mindful Walking

Walk slowly and deliberately, feeling each step. Notice the ground beneath your feet, the air on your skin.

⏱️ 5 minutes 🎯 Beginner
